MA RCA PROJECT

(CONCEPT & INNOVATION PROJECT FOR WINTER SPORTS)

01.BASE LAYER + PROTECTION INNOVATION

(PATENT PENDING PROJECT)

This project introduces a new category of winter sportswear: a technical baselayer engineered for mobility, adaptability, and comfort. Designed in response to the physical demands of winter sports and the inefficiency of traditional layering, it integrates thermal regulation, sweat management, and soft-impact protection into a single, seamless knitted garment.

Using advanced digital knitting technology (Shima Seiki), the garment embeds anatomically mapped zones of breathability, support, and structural support‚Äîeach responding to the body's physiological needs during intense motion. By unifying these functions within one textile system, it reduces bulk while enhancing performance for both athletes and travellers.

A key innovation lies in customizable protective zones. Lightweight containers are strategically placed at high-impact areas such as the spine, shoulders, and elbows. Users can inject a soft silicone material that hardens in 20 minutes, forming a personalised protective shell that conforms to the wearer's unique contours.

02.SHOES PROJECT

(CORDWAINERS FOOTWEAR AWARDS 2025 FINALIST)

Building on the baselayer innovation, this footwear project extends the same philosophy of adaptable protection and user-centered design into winter sports footwear. The concept explores modular cushioning systems and anatomical fit technologies that respond to the varied demands of snow sports environments.

The design integrates lightweight materials with strategic reinforcement zones, allowing athletes to customize support levels based on their discipline‚Äîwhether alpine skiing, snowboarding, or backcountry exploration. The project was recognized as a finalist for The Stuart Weitzman Award for Best MA at the Cordwainers Footwear Awards 2025.
