MA RCA PROJECT

(CONCEPT & INNOVATION PROJECT FOR WINTER SPORTS)

01.BASE LAYER + PROTECTION INNOVATION

(PATENT PENDING PROJECT)

This project introduces a new category of winter sportswear: a technical baselayer engineered for mobility, adaptability, and comfort. Designed in response to the physical demands of winter sports and the inefficiency of traditional layering, it integrates thermal regulation, sweat management, and soft-impact protection into a single, seamless knitted garment.

Using advanced digital knitting technology (Shima Seiki), the garment embeds anatomically mapped zones of breathability, support, and structural support‚Äîeach responding to the body's physiological needs during intense motion. By unifying these functions within one textile system, it reduces bulk while enhancing performance for both athletes and travellers.

A key innovation lies in customizable protective zones. Lightweight containers are strategically placed at high-impact areas such as the spine, shoulders, and elbows. Users can inject a soft silicone material that hardens in 20 minutes, forming a personalised protective shell that conforms to the wearer's unique contours. This participatory approach empowers athletes to engineer their own gear, merging function with individual agency.

Material choices were informed by research into the Comfort Properties of Fabric for Winter Sportswear, mapping fibre content and knit structure against insulation, elasticity, and moisture control. Functional yarns were selected for targeted performance‚Äîsuch as wicking fibres at sweat zones, insulating yarns at cold-exposed areas, and elastic fibres for freedom of movement.

Rather than relying on multiple garments to achieve warmth, protection, and moisture balance, this baselayer consolidates them into a singular performance tool. It proposes a modular, intelligent textile system that adapts to the evolving needs of the body in motion, reimagining the future of winter sportswear as lighter, smarter, and more user-driven.
