MA RCA Technical Baselayer

MA FASHION FOOTWEAR & ACCESSORIES DESIGN

This project introduces a new category of winter sportswear: a technical baselayer engineered for mobility, adaptability, and comfort. Designed in response to the physical demands of winter sports and the inefficiency of traditional layering, it integrates thermal regulation, sweat management, and soft-impact protection into a single, seamless knitted garment.

Advanced digital knitting technology (Shima Seiki) maps zones of breathability, compression, and structural support—each responding to the body's physiological needs during intense motion. By unifying these functions within one textile system, it reduces bulk while elevating performance for athletes and travellers alike.

A key innovation lies in customizable protective zones. Lightweight containers are strategically placed at high-impact areas such as the spine, shoulders, and elbows. Users inject a soft silicone material that cures within 20 minutes, forming a personalised protective shell tailored to the wearer.

Shoes Project

CORDWAINERS FOOTWEAR AWARDS 2025 – STUART WEITZMAN AWARD FINALIST

Building on the baselayer innovation, this footwear project extends the same philosophy of adaptable protection and user-centred design into winter sports footwear. The concept explores modular cushioning systems and anatomical fit technologies that respond to the varied demands of snow sports environments.

The design integrates lightweight materials with strategic reinforcement zones, allowing athletes to customise support levels based on discipline—whether alpine skiing, snowboarding, or backcountry exploration. Recognised as a finalist for The Stuart Weitzman Award for Best MA, the work highlights both technical fluency and aesthetic restraint.

IFF × RCA Collaboration

SCENT & NARRATIVE DESIGN

A collaborative exploration between fashion design and olfactory experience, investigating how scent can enhance the narrative of wearable design. The project bridges sensory disciplines, creating immersive storytelling that engages both body and atmosphere.

Research examines the interplay between fragrance diffusion and garment architecture, translating scent into spatial cues that evolve as the wearer moves.
